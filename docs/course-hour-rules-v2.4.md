# SAT course-hour rules — approved v2.4

This document is the consolidated policy implemented by the package.

1. Every one of the 28 lessons is included and receives at least 0.25 live hour.
2. Module 1 questions weigh 1.00 and Module B questions weigh 1.20. Difficulty labels do not alter weight.
3. Weighted lesson accuracy controls the performance multiplier; coverage creates a floor that prevents unsupported reductions.
4. Each lesson uses its approved lesson-specific calibrated base hour from the catalog. Breadth, difficulty, and strategy scores form the explanatory rubric profile; no universal sum-to-hours conversion is used.
5. Foundational evidence applies a 1.25 or 1.50 override through the core-hours maximum formula. With no foundational concern, core hours are simply base × final multiplier, so legitimate diagnostic reductions remain possible.
6. Additions are scaled to the new hour model: weak clusters +0.25/+0.50/+0.75; timing +0.25/+0.25/+0.50; evidence-gated high target +0.25.
7. High-target time cannot be triggered by limited or absent coverage alone.
8. Lesson values are not rounded. Only the final total is rounded upward to the next 0.25 hour.
9. Individual program range is 14–35 live hours. The engine modifies actual components to meet this range.
10. Orientation is fixed at a minimum/default of 0.75 hour. Final review defaults to 1.50 hours and may be reduced no lower than 1.00 hour.
11. Maximum enforcement uses Stage 1 remediation transfer, Stage 2 optional strategy/final-review reduction, Stage 3 strongest-lesson compression, and Stage 4 exact-remainder removal. All lessons keep at least 0.25 hour.
12. Homework is proportional to final live lesson hours and separate from live instruction.
13. Group strategy, limits, and final rounding are aligned with the individual rules. A one-student group equals an individual case.
14. Reports show the unrounded component total and final scheduled total separately.
